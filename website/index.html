Privacy by design

Your keys stay on your device

SnowRaven Mini has no account, no analytics, no telemetry, and no server. You bring your own two free API keys, for eBird and OpenWeather. They are stored only in your browser and each is sent only to its own service. NOAA tide data needs no key. There is nothing in the middle.

  • You bring the keysTwo free keys, entered once on the Settings page.
  • No trackingZero analytics or telemetry, in either browser.
  • Keys stay localStored in chrome.storage.local, sent only to each API.
  • No developer serverThere is no SnowRaven Mini cloud. Nothing to run.

What it does

Weather and tide, without leaving eBird

One click on a checklist resolves where and when you were, fetches the weather and tide, and hands them to you ready to paste.

One-click weather and tide

Open the popup on a checklist's Edit Comments page and the weather block copies itself, in the same format the SnowRaven desktop app produces. The tide for the nearest NOAA station sits right below with its own copy button. Switch to the comment field and paste.

Your keys, your device

Paste your two free keys once on the Settings page. They live in your browser's local storage and are sent only to eBird and OpenWeather, never to anyone else. There is no account and no server.

Asks only for what it needs

SnowRaven Mini reaches three APIs, eBird, OpenWeather, and NOAA, and nothing else. It requests that access from your click the first time you use it, not at install, so there is no broad-permission warning and no surprise.

Also get the tides

Tide is for US coastal checklists. When the nearest NOAA station is too far away or your checklist is inland, SnowRaven Mini says so plainly, with an override to show the nearest station anyway, rather than inventing a number.

What you'll need

Two free API keys, entered once in Settings:

  • eBird API key, free from ebird.org/api/keygen.
  • OpenWeather API key, free from openweathermap.org, on the One Call by Call plan.

NOAA tide data needs no key. Your keys never leave your device.

A small companion to SnowRaven, shared freely

SnowRaven Mini does one job: the weather and tide comment blocks, in your browser, the same way the SnowRaven desktop app makes them. It is open source and free, with nothing to sign up for and nothing to buy.
